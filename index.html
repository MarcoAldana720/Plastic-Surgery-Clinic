¿Is this a free clinic?

Visage is not a free clinic. We are a non-profit community plastic surgery clinic. We offer a sliding fee scale to uninsured patients based upon family size and income.

¿What free services do you have?

We offer several state funded programs that provide free services if you are eligible. Eligibility is based on family size and income. We can also provide information regarding region’s health insurance exchange. Please call the clinic for more details about these programs.

¿Do i need an appointment?

Yes, we prefer that you have an appointment but same-day appointments are available.

¿Do you accept checks?

No, please note, that we accept cash, Visa, MasterCard, American Express and Discover Card.

Reconstructive Surgery



From the top of the head to the tip of the toe, and from newborn babies to the very elderly, reconstructive surgery is used to treat a wide range of conditions. It is all about repairing people and restoring function. It is performed to repair and reshape bodily structures affected by birth defects, developmental abnormalities, trauma/injuries, infections, tumours and disease.

Microsurgery



Microsurgical procedures are performed on parts of the body that are best visualized under a microscope. Examples of such structures are small blood vessels, nerves, and tubes. It uses techniques that have been performed by surgeons since the early XXth century.

Craniofacial surgery



Craniofacial surgery is a type of plastic surgery that deals with the correction and reconstruction of malformations or problems of the skull. Our highly experienced surgeons can solve any issue of this concern.

Reconstructive Surgery


A surgical specialty focusing on correcting skull, face, and jaw deformities caused by trauma or congenital conditions like cleft lip, palate, or craniosynostosis, aiming to restore function, symmetry, and aesthetics.


Microsurgery


A surgical technique involving the use of a microscope to perform intricate procedures on small structures like blood vessels and nerves. It is essential in reattaching limbs, repairing nerves, and transplanting tissue, such as free flap reconstructions in cancer or trauma patients.


Craniofacial surgery


A branch of surgery focused on restoring function and appearance after trauma, disease, or congenital anomalies. It includes procedures like skin grafts, tissue transfers, and scar revisions, often overlapping with craniofacial and microsurgery techniques.
